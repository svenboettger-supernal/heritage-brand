The Mark

The lockup is a heraldic asset.

Use the supplied lockup as a single image. Do not redraw it, do not recolor it, do not set it inside a container. The wax seal carries the firm's only red. It does not appear anywhere else in the system.

On navy · white pill

Single-image rule

The lockup is supplied as a file. Do not recreate it in code, do not letterspace the wordmark, do not rebuild the seal.

Clearspace

Reserve the cap height of the H in HERITAGE around the entire lockup. Nothing crosses that margin.

Minimum size

Full lockup at least 180px wide on screen, 32mm in print. Below that, use the wax-seal monogram alone.

On dark grounds

The lockup sits on a white pill, not directly on navy. The seal needs light around it to read.

Typography

One serif, one sans, one mono.

Source Serif 4 for display and headlines. Inter for body, navigation, and dense UI. JetBrains Mono for account references, ticker numbers, and dates. No fourth typeface, no decorative font, no display swashes.

Comparison

Heritage versus the alternatives.

A three-way pattern, borrowed in shape from modern advisory sites and rendered in the Heritage palette. The third card carries the lockup.

Generic Trust Company

A category, not a relationship.

  • Single point of contact rotates every 18 months
  • Off-the-shelf trust documents
  • Quarterly statements, no narrative
  • Cross-sold investment products

Single-Family Office

Yours alone, with the cost that implies.

  • Internal hire and retention burden on the family
  • Operational risk concentrated in one staff
  • No peer benchmarking across families
  • Succession risk when the principal exits

Heritage

A stewardship, structured for three generations.

  • Named partner, named successor, on file from day one
  • Stewardship plan reviewed at each generational handover
  • Quarterly statement with a written letter from the partner
  • Aligned compensation, no product cross-sell

The brand is the stewardship, not the strategy. Every design decision answers to a single question, would a custodian holding a family's wealth across three generations recognise this as their own.

House Rules

Do, do not.

Do

  • Default to the cool near-white ground. The brand was designed for it.
  • Use Source Serif 4 for display and Inter for everything else.
  • Reserve navy for headlines, nav, the filled CTA, and one mid-page band.
  • Pair every numeric figure with tabular figures and JetBrains Mono.
  • Use the supplied lockup as a single image. Do not recreate it.
  • Write in declarative, owner-dated, next-step framed prose.
  • Anchor the founding year, family count, and assets-under-stewardship high on the page.

Do not

  • Do not introduce red. The wax seal in the logo is the only red the brand carries.
  • Do not paint a button, a badge, or a link burgundy.
  • Do not put the full page on a dark ground. Dark is for one mid-page band and the splash mode.
  • Do not introduce a third typeface, a swash serif, or a display script.
  • Do not use stock wealth photography. Commission a real portrait or use type alone.
  • Do not write em dashes. Use commas, periods, or parentheses.
  • Do not write claims like "we believe" or "premier". Numbers and names beat adjectives.
  • Do not redraw the wax seal in CSS. The logo is supplied as a file.
