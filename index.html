Heritage Strategies LLC · Brand System v1

Stewardship across generations.

A deep midnight navy ground. A wax-oxblood identity seal. Two bronze hairlines. Two fonts. A single luminous arc. A steel cobalt accent reserved for state. The brand is the stewardship, not the strategy.

Section · 01 · The Mark

A family crest, pressed

Wax seal + two-line wordmark, separated by a bronze hairline

The identity is a soft circular wax disc pressed into the ground, paired with a Trajan-style serif lockup. HERITAGE sits above STRATEGIES LLC, joined by a single thin bronze hairline that runs the width of the wordmark. The seal is always oxblood. The wordmark is two-color: cream on dark, navy on parchment. Never recolor either. Never set the lockup inside a containing shape.

On dark · #0e1d33

On parchment · #f4ede0

Two-color rule

Wax-oxblood seal + cream wordmark on dark. Wax-oxblood seal + navy wordmark on parchment. Never solid oxblood, never solid white, never gradients.

The bronze hairline

The 1px bronze rule between HERITAGE and STRATEGIES LLC is part of the wordmark. Never omit it, never thicken it, never recolor it.

Clearspace

Reserve the cap-height of the H in HERITAGE around the entire lockup. Nothing crosses that boundary — not a luminous arc, not adjacent type.

Minimum size

Full lockup ≥ 180px wide. Seal alone ≥ 32px square. Below that, hand-set HS monogram, never a downscaled raster.

Never alter

No stretching, rotating, recoloring, outlining, embossing beyond the seal's natural impression, drop-shadows, or containing shapes.

Never animate

The seal does not spin, fade in, pulse, or "stamp down" on page load. A signature does not animate.

Section · 02 · Typography

Two fonts, archival voice

Classical display serif + clean modern sans

Tiempos Headline does the heavy lifting for the wordmark, headings, hero copy, and editorial body on letterhead. Söhne handles navigation, body, and all dense data. If brand-licensed fonts are unavailable, Cormorant Garamond and Inter are mandatory free fallbacks. JetBrains Mono is allowed for account references and code only. Three fonts in a Heritage surface is a redesign, not a variation.

Section · 03 · Color

Seven swatches, strict roles

No improvisation, no crossovers

Oxblood is identity, never interaction. Bronze is a hairline color, never a fill. Cobalt is state, never decoration. Deep midnight navy is the page; cards descend toward black, not ascend toward white. Parchment is for document interiors, never a marketing-page ground. The system breaks the moment any color crosses its lane.

Oxblood is identity

The wax seal, the section-opening pip, the active-nav-item underline. Never paint a button oxblood by default. Filled-oxblood is reserved for one hero CTA per page.

Bronze is a hairline

1px structural rules and small-caps eyebrow color only. Bronze cannot carry a fill at any reasonable area without losing its metallic register.

Cobalt is state

Active, selected, focus, info. The moment cobalt becomes decoration, its meaning as a state indicator is broken everywhere else.

Cards go darker

On the navy ground, cards descend toward Quiet Slate, not ascend toward white. The page reads like ink absorbing into paper.

Section · 04 · Visual Accents

Three sanctioned motifs

The seal · the arc · the bronze hairlines

Three decorative devices, all drawn from the wax-seal Zoom card. The seal is the signature, used in the lockup and at the top of letterhead. The luminous arc is the watermark, used once per viewport at low opacity. The bronze hairlines are the structural frame at top and bottom of every full-page surface. Nothing else is invented.

Motif · 01 · The wax seal

Motif · 02 · The luminous arc

The wax seal

Pressed, irregular, deeper at the edges. Always oxblood. Once per viewport. Letterhead upper-left, the lockup, the PDF section opener. Never recolored, never animated, never in a containing shape.

The luminous arc

A single silver-cream hairline arc sweeping the lower half of dark surfaces. 8–15% opacity. Once per viewport. Never on parchment. Never animated.

Bronze hairlines

1px bronze rule beneath the nav. 1px bronze rule above the footer. The structural frame on every full-page surface. Heritage's quieter equivalent of a leather binding.

Section · 06 · House Rules

Do · Don't

Restraint over volume

Do

  • Default to the deep midnight navy ground. The brand was designed for it.
  • Use exactly two fonts — display serif and sans. Mono for account references only.
  • Keep the bronze hairlines at the top and the bottom of every full-page surface.
  • Open every section with the bronze eyebrow + 56px bronze rule + serif heading + sans subheading.
  • Treat the wax seal as a signature. Once per viewport at most. Always oxblood.
  • Treat the luminous arc as a watermark. Once per viewport, low opacity, never animated.
  • Reserve Steel Cobalt for state — active, selected, focus, info.
  • Use tabular figures in any dense data. Old-style figures in editorial serif prose.
  • Show the principal. Named, with credentials. Real face, available light.
  • Write declaratively. Owner, date, next step. Speak like a trustee writing a quarterly letter.

Don't

  • Don't recolor the wax seal. It is oxblood, always. No navy version, no white version.
  • Don't omit the bronze hairline between HERITAGE and STRATEGIES LLC. It is part of the wordmark.
  • Don't paint a button oxblood by default. Bronze-outline is the default; filled-oxblood is the hero CTA.
  • Don't paint a button bronze. Bronze is a hairline color, not a fill.
  • Don't use cobalt as decoration. State only.
  • Don't make cards lighter than the page. Cards go darker, not lighter.
  • Don't use parchment as a marketing-page ground. Parchment is for document interiors.
  • Don't use stock wealth imagery — skylines, handshakes, "advisor at conference table."
  • Don't write "we believe," "we are passionate about," "experience true wealth," or "schedule a discovery call."
  • Don't use em dashes. Heritage tone. Use a comma, a period, parentheses, or a colon.
  • Don't animate the wax seal or the luminous arc. Signatures do not animate.
